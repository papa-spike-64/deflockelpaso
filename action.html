Take Action

The contract expires May 16.
Make your voice heard now.

El Paso is not Flock's customer.
El Paso residents are the product.

We are calling on the El Paso City Council to cancel the Flock Safety contract and not renew it. We oppose this vendor and any similar private, venture-backed surveillance company that profits from the data of El Paso residents. If the city insists on renewing despite public opposition, we demand the minimum conditions listed on our facts page — but our position is clear: the contract should end on May 16.

Why Removal — Not Reform

The business model is the problem

Flock Safety is not a public safety company that happens to collect data. It is a data company that uses public safety as its entry point. Understanding their business model explains everything.

$7,500,000,000
Flock Safety's valuation — a Georgia startup with $950 million in venture capital funding
$4,683
What El Paso paid per camera per year — 150 cameras, $702,500 total grant. For 24/7 surveillance of an entire major city.

Ask yourself: how does a company justify a $7.5 billion valuation by charging less than $5,000 per camera per year? The hardware isn't the product. The network is. Every city Flock enters adds to a massive, interconnected database of vehicle movements across America. That database — your routes, your patterns, your life — is what investors are valuing at $7.5 billion.

El Paso didn't buy a public safety tool. El Paso gave a private company free access to photograph every vehicle in our city, 24 hours a day, in exchange for hardware that costs less than a used car per year.

This is not a vendor accountability problem that better contract terms can fix. This is a business model problem. The only solution is to not participate in it.

What we support instead

If El Paso wants license plate reader technology, it should be locally governed and locally accountable — owned and controlled by the city, under ordinance, with elected officials answerable to residents. Not a venture-backed startup in Georgia that needs to justify a $7.5 billion valuation to its investors. Public safety tools should serve the public — not a private data network.

How to Take Action

Three ways to make an impact

Start with whichever feels easiest. All three together take less than 15 minutes.

  • Call your city council representative. A phone call carries more weight than an email. Use the script below. It takes 2 minutes. You don't need to know everything — just read the script.
  • Send an email. Copy the email template below and send it directly to your representative. If you don't know your district, use the city's district finder.
  • Speak at public comment. City Council meets most Monday mornings. Any El Paso resident can speak for 3 minutes. Sign up by Tuesday at 9am for that week's meeting. Details below.
Script — Phone Call

What to say when you call

You'll likely reach a staffer, not the representative directly. That's fine. Staffers log every call and report them. Be polite, be clear, and stick to the script.

Phone script — 2 minutes
"Hi, my name is [YOUR NAME] and I'm a resident of El Paso, District [YOUR DISTRICT]. I'm calling about the Flock Safety license plate reader contract, which expires on May 16th. I want [REPRESENTATIVE NAME] to know that I am opposed to renewing this contract. Flock Safety has a documented record of security failures, unauthorized data access, and quietly rewriting its own terms to remove data protections. More than 30 cities across the country have already canceled their Flock contracts. El Paso residents are not Flock's customer — we are their product. Our movements, our patterns, our data — that's what funds a $7.5 billion valuation for a private company we never agreed to work for. I'm asking [REPRESENTATIVE NAME] to vote against renewing the Flock contract when it comes before the council, and to support locally governed alternatives that keep El Paso's data in El Paso hands. Can I count on [REPRESENTATIVE NAME] to oppose this renewal? Thank you for your time."
Replace [YOUR NAME], [YOUR DISTRICT], and [REPRESENTATIVE NAME] with your information before calling.
Script — Email

What to send

Keep it short. Council staff read hundreds of emails. A focused, personal message is more effective than a long one.

Email — subject line
Please vote NO on Flock Safety contract renewal — May 16 expiration
Email — body
Dear [REPRESENTATIVE NAME], My name is [YOUR NAME] and I am a resident of El Paso, District [YOUR DISTRICT]. I am writing to ask you to oppose the renewal of the City's contract with Flock Safety, which expires on May 16, 2026. My concerns are straightforward: Flock Safety has a documented record of security failures — 22 confirmed vulnerabilities in the federal cybersecurity database, and a history of enabling unauthorized federal access to local data in cities across the country without the knowledge of local police departments. In February 2026, Flock quietly rewrote its Terms of Service with 147 changes, including deleting the clause that previously stated they would not sell customer data. El Paso PD's own transparency portal lists a Harris County commissioner's office — not a law enforcement agency — as having access to our camera data, with no public explanation. El Paso paid less than $5,000 per camera per year for 150 cameras that photograph every vehicle in our city, 24 hours a day. That price only makes sense if our data is the real product. Flock Safety is valued at $7.5 billion. El Paso residents are not their customer — we are their inventory. More than 30 cities have already canceled their Flock contracts. El Paso should join them. I am asking you to vote against renewing this contract, and to support an open process to evaluate locally governed alternatives that keep El Paso data under El Paso control. Thank you for your service to our community. Respectfully, [YOUR NAME] [YOUR ADDRESS / NEIGHBORHOOD] [YOUR PHONE — optional]

Speak Up In Person

How to speak at City Council public comment

You have the right to speak. Here's how.

El Paso City Council meets most Monday mornings at City Hall, 300 N. Campbell. Any resident can speak during public comment — you do not need to be invited, you do not need to be an expert, and you do not need to give your address.

To sign up: Contact the City Clerk's office by Tuesday at 9am the week of the meeting you want to attend. Call (915) 212-0049 or email cityclerk@elpasotexas.gov

You get 3 minutes. Stick to the facts. Be respectful. End with a clear ask: "I am asking this council to vote against renewing the Flock Safety contract."

Need Spanish interpretation? Email cityclerk@elpasotexas.gov by noon the Friday before the meeting to request it at no cost.

Pro tip: Print and bring copies of the facts page to hand to council members and staff. A physical document in someone's hand is harder to ignore than a website.

What to say in 3 minutes

State your name and district. Say you oppose the Flock renewal. Give one or two specific facts — the 22 security vulnerabilities, the Harris County data access, the $7.5 billion valuation built on resident data. End with your ask. Sit down. That's it. One person speaking clearly and calmly does more than ten people shouting.
